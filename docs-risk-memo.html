Risk Memo

A short decision memo framing the case study as lending-risk operations work instead of a competition artifact.

Objective

Use the Home Credit public dataset as a proxy consumer lending book and show how an explainable scorecard can support origination policy, portfolio monitoring, and borrower-level review.

The intent is not to present a Kaggle submission story. The intent is to show what a practical lending-risk stack looks like when translated into policy and governance language.

Lifecycle View
Pre-Loan

Rank applications with an explainable scorecard and map scores into approve, manual review, and decline bands.

In-Loan

Monitor PSI, vintage seasoning, calibration, and bad rate by score decile.

Post-Loan

Use borrower traces to inspect late-payment history, refusal patterns, and bureau stress.

Fraud Extension

Add application velocity, identity consistency, bureau inquiry bursts, and device/session anomalies as the next overlay.

Policy Stance
ApproveLower-risk score bands, subject to hard-policy checks outside the model.
Manual ReviewMid-risk bands, missing-document review, or affordability verification.
DeclineHighest-risk bands unless a challenger or analyst override justifies a second look.
Monitoring Triggers
PSIWatch at 0.10, escalate at 0.25.
VintageReview latest cohorts when seasoning materially worsens against trailing reference vintages.
ChallengerLaunch only when benchmark uplift is material and calibration does not worsen.
Governance
ExplainabilityReason codes come from the same bins used for scoring.
Proxy RiskOccupation, organization, application-time, and sparse document features stay out of the policy-facing scorecard.
MissingnessMissingness is treated as portfolio structure, not just preprocessing noise.
